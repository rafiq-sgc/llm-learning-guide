What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think and learn like humans. The term was coined in 1956 at the Dartmouth Conference.

History of AI

Timeline:

  • 1950s: Alan Turing proposes the Turing Test
  • 1956: Dartmouth Conference - AI as a field is born
  • 1960s-70s: Early AI programs (ELIZA, SHRDLU)
  • 1980s: Expert systems and knowledge-based AI
  • 1990s: Machine learning gains prominence
  • 2000s: Big data and improved algorithms
  • 2010s: Deep learning revolution
  • 2020s: Large Language Models and Generative AI

Types of AI

  1. Narrow AI (Weak AI): Designed for specific tasks
  • Examples: Chess programs, voice assistants, image recognition
  1. General AI (Strong AI): Human-level intelligence across all tasks
  • Still theoretical, not yet achieved
  1. Superintelligence: AI that surpasses human intelligence
  • Future concept

AI Approaches

ğŸ“Š Understanding This AI Approaches Diagram:

What does this diagram show? This diagram illustrates the three main approaches to Artificial Intelligence and how they branch into different subcategories. It shows the evolution from traditional rule-based systems to modern Large Language Models.

ğŸ” Step-by-Step Breakdown:
  1. Starting Point - Artificial Intelligence (Blue): This is the root of all AI approaches. Everything branches from here.
  2. Three Main Branches:
    • Symbolic AI (Top Branch): The oldest approach, based on logic and rules
      • Rule-based Systems: Uses explicit if-then rules (e.g., "If temperature > 100, then fever")
      • Expert Systems: Mimics human expert knowledge in specific domains (e.g., medical diagnosis systems)
    • Statistical AI (Middle Branch): Uses probability and statistics
      • Machine Learning: Learns patterns from data without explicit programming
      • Probabilistic Models: Uses probability distributions to make predictions
    • Connectionist AI (Green - Most Modern): Based on neural networks
      • Neural Networks: Inspired by the human brain, uses interconnected nodes
      • Deep Learning: Neural networks with many layers (hence "deep")
      • Large Language Models (Yellow - Latest): The most advanced form, like GPT, ChatGPT, etc.
  3. Color Coding:
    • Blue = Root concept (AI itself)
    • Green = Modern connectionist approach
    • Yellow = Latest development (LLMs)

ğŸ’¡ Key Takeaway: Follow the arrows from "Artificial Intelligence" to see how AI has evolved from simple rule-based systems to complex Large Language Models. The green and yellow branches represent the most modern and powerful approaches used today.

ğŸ¯ Real-World Example: When you use ChatGPT, you're interacting with a Large Language Model, which is part of the Connectionist AI branch. It uses Deep Learning (neural networks with many layers) to understand and generate human-like text.
